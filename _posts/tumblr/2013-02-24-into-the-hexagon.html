My introduction to Super Hexagon was when Verge came out and in the “play this” section was featured a flash game called “Hexagon” (wow, its almost an year!), simple, eye-catching yet feverishly difficult. Made by Terry Cavanagh, this game caught attention of many people for its minimalism and retro style.

                       

The basic premise of the game is to navigate a little triangle in the center by moving it clockwise or anti-clockwise, through obstacles, as they head towards you. did i mention the music? Awesome retro video game music accompanies as you die countless times. And it eventually grows on you.

Why is it called hexagon? It has 6 levels of 10 seconds(former 3) and 15 seconds(latter 2) and last level(Hexagon) is indefinite(goes on, until you eventually die). And the obstacles, they are open polygons well.. not exactly, but you know.., killer geometrical shapes. 

Go play it! Disclaimer: its seriously addictive! no kidding! 

p.s.: my high score (hexagon) is 85 sec, pretty ok, i guess.. hehe..
