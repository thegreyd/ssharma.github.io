30th June 2012 was a second longer, and Atomic clocks all over the world read 23:59:60 for a second.

This ’leap second’ adjustment was first introduced in 1972, and have been used 24 times since then, and this was the 25thÂ occasion. Because the Earth’s rotational speed varies, due to varying climatic and geological events; these adjustments keep international time close to accuracy.
